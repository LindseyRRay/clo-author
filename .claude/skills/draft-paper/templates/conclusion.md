# Conclusion Template

**Target length:** 500–800 words. ~1 page.
**Position in paper:** Last section.

## Required elements

- Restate the main finding with effect size (rule 27).
- 1–2 sentences on each supporting finding.
- Honest limitations paragraph (NOT defensive).
- Implications: for policy, for the field, or both.
- Closing sentence with the so-what claim.

## Paragraph-by-paragraph scaffold

### Paragraph 1 — Restate the main finding (~150 words)
- Topic sentence: the headline finding with magnitude and units.
- 2–3 sentences walking the supporting findings, also with magnitudes.
- Closing sentence: bridges to interpretation.

### Paragraph 2 — Interpretation / what it means (~150 words)
- Topic sentence: the so-what.
- Why the findings matter for the broader question raised in the introduction.
- If a framework was developed in the paper, this paragraph maps findings back to framework predictions.

### Paragraph 3 — Limitations (~100–150 words)
- Direct, not hedging. The reader should trust the limitations more than the findings if anything.
- 2–3 specific limitations:
  - "Our data covers [X but not Y]."
  - "We cannot distinguish A from B."
  - "External validity to Z is limited because …"
- Closing sentence: how the limitations affect interpretation, not whether the paper is "valid."

### Paragraph 4 — Implications and so-what (~150–200 words)
- Implications for policy, if applicable.
- Implications for academic literature.
- Implications for practitioners.
- Closing sentence: one-line so-what that mirrors the paper's title or research question.

### Paragraph 5 (optional) — Future research (~50–100 words)
- 1–2 sentences. Pointers, not commitments.
- DO NOT write "future research should …" — instead, name what is unknown and why.
- Many strong papers omit this paragraph. Skip if you have nothing new to say.

## Banned patterns

- "In conclusion, …" → drop and lead with the finding.
- "More research is needed." → useless. Replace with a specific question that remains open.
- "This paper contributes to …" → already done in the intro.
- "Our results have important implications." → name the implication.
- Restating the abstract verbatim.

## Voice

- Confident on findings. Honest on limitations.
- Don't oversell; don't undersell.
- Active voice.

## Length norms from example papers
- Generative AI at Work: conclusion ~700 words, 4 paragraphs.
- Market Effects of Algorithms: conclusion ~650 words, 4 paragraphs.
- Hiring as Exploration: conclusion ~600 words, 3 paragraphs.
