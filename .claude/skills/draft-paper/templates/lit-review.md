# Literature Review Template

**Target length:** 600–1,200 words.
**Position in paper:** Either (a) integrated into the introduction (preferred for short papers), or (b) standalone Section 2 (preferred when literature is dense and the paper engages multiple distinct strands).
**Reads first:** Annotated bibliography from the Librarian agent if available.

## Required elements

- Thematic organization (NOT chronological, NOT alphabetical).
- Comparatives that position the paper against the cited work.
- Citations integrated into prose, serving the argument.
- Acknowledgment of mixed evidence where it exists.

## Structure

### Opening paragraph (~100 words)
- One sentence naming the literatures the paper engages.
- One-to-two sentences describing the consensus or tension in those literatures.
- Closing sentence: "We contribute to this literature by …" (only if not already covered in the intro contribution paragraph — otherwise skip this sentence to avoid duplication).

### Theme 1 (~150–300 words)
- Topic sentence naming the theme.
- 2–4 sentences integrating relevant papers using comparatives:
  - "Consistent with our findings, [Author1, Author2, and Author3 (Year)] find that …"
  - "By contrast, [Author (Year)] reports …"
  - "Closely related, [Author and Author (Year)] study … and find …"
- Closing sentence: how our paper relates — what we add, where we agree, where we differ.

### Theme 2 (~150–300 words)
- Same template as Theme 1.

### Theme 3 (~150–300 words, optional)
- Same template.

### Closing paragraph (~50–100 words)
- One-to-two sentences summarizing what is new about our contribution relative to these themes.
- DO NOT use "This paper contributes to the literature on X" as a standalone framing.

## Banned patterns

- Chronological list: "Author 1 (1990) studies X. Author 2 (1995) studies Y. Author 3 (2000) finds Z."
- Alphabetical list of citations without prose.
- 5+ parenthetical citations without prose connecting them (citation dump).
- Standalone "This paper contributes to …" sentence.
- Long quotations from cited papers.

## Comparative phrases to use

| Relationship | Phrase |
|--------------|--------|
| Agree with | "Consistent with …, we find …" |
| Disagree with | "In contrast to …, our results show …" |
| Extend | "We build on … by examining …" |
| Close cousin | "Closely related, … studies …" |
| Distinguish from | "Unlike …, we exploit …" |
| Acknowledge mixed evidence | "Evidence is mixed on …: while … find …, others … find …" |

## Length norms from example papers
- Generative AI at Work: dedicated Section 2.2 ~750 words, 3 themes.
- Market Effects of Algorithms: lit review integrated into Section 1 (intro), ~600 words.
- Hiring as Exploration: dedicated Section 2 ~1,100 words, 4 themes.
